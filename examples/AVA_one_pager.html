The Problem

Shared data is a copy
you can't take back.

The moment you hand over a dataset, a model, or a deal file, you lose control of it. It can be saved, forwarded, and re-shared — to people you never approved, for as long as they choose to keep it. A copy is forever, and it is no longer yours.

One copy
becomes many

Every recipient can duplicate your file instantly — and you'll never know how many exist.

Forwarded
past your reach

Once it leaves your hands it travels on its own — through inboxes and drives you can't see.

No way
to revoke

You can't un-send what you've already given. Access, once granted, is permanent.

There is another way ▼
AVA

Share the value.
Keep the only copy.

AVA lets you give a counterparty access to your data without ever handing over a copy that can be forwarded. You stay the owner. They get access — not the file. And you can withdraw it at any time.

You keep the only copy

The data never leaves your control. Recipients work with it in place — they never hold it.

Access by revocable key

Grant access with a key — and cut it off the moment the relationship or the deal changes.

Attested, not trusted

Access runs inside verified, sealed compute. Control is proven cryptographically — not promised.

Nothing to forward

There is no loose file to leak, copy, or re-share. Remove the copy, and you remove the risk.
